What you can control:

  • Change your password at any time in Settings
  • Delete your account and all associated data at any time (see Section 7.2)

6. Data Storage and Security
6.1 Where Your Data Is Stored: On-Device vs. Server

Your account information and health tracking data (symptoms, cycle phase, mood/energy logs, program progress) are stored on secure servers, not only on your device.

What's stored on our servers:

  • Your account email and hashed password
  • Your onboarding profile (hormonal stage, symptoms, goals, preferences)
  • Daily tracking data (energy, mood, sleep quality, anxiety scores, symptom logs)
  • Habit completion history and program progress
  • AI coach conversation history, so your coach can maintain context between sessions

Why we store this on a server rather than only on your device: Server storage lets you see your patterns and progress over time, means you won't lose your data if you switch phones or reinstall the App, and allows your AI coach to give continuity-aware advice rather than starting from zero every conversation.

What stays local to your device only: Biometric data used to unlock the App (such as Face ID or Touch ID) is handled entirely by your device's operating system and is never sent to our servers. Unsaved drafts (such as a partially typed journal entry) also remain local until you save them.

Your data is stored on servers located in the United States. If you are accessing the App from outside the United States, please be aware that your information will be transferred to and processed in the United States, subject to the international transfer safeguards described in Section 7.5.

6.2 Security Measures and the Reasoning Behind Them

We implement industry-standard technical and organizational security measures, and we want to explain the reasoning behind our key choices rather than simply listing them:

  • Encryption in transit and at rest: All data moving between your device and our servers is encrypted (TLS 1.2+), and all data is encrypted at rest in our database.
  • Row-level security: Access controls are enforced at the database level, not just in App code. This means the database itself blocks any attempt to access another user's data — a structural safeguard, not merely a policy.
  • Why we don't store payment information ourselves: Subscription payments are processed by Apple, Google, or RevenueCat, not by us directly. We never see or store your full payment card number, which reduces the sensitive data we hold and the risk if our systems were ever compromised.
  • Why we use an established AI provider: We use Anthropic's Claude API to power AI coaching rather than building our own model, because doing so allows us to rely on an established provider's security and safety practices rather than the more error-prone approach of a small team securing custom infrastructure from scratch.
  • Restricted access: Access to production data is limited to essential personnel and logged.
  • Regular security reviews.

No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

📉
During perimenopause and menopause, estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone all decline — starting as early as the mid-30s for testosterone. This affects hot flashes, night sweats, sleep, mood, energy, libido, brain fog, bone density, and more.
💊
Bioidentical HRT replaces those hormones in forms your body recognizes — including estrogen gel, testosterone gel, and micronized progesterone. These are not the same as older synthetic hormones like Premarin (made from horse urine) or synthetic progestins — and the distinction matters both for safety and effectiveness.
🔬
HRT is one of the most studied medical interventions in women's health. Guidance has changed significantly since the 2002 WHI study that caused widespread fear.
⚡
Testosterone often gets left out of this conversation, but it is worth raising directly — low energy and reduced libido are two of the most common, and most overlooked, signs of declining testosterone. See the "Types" tab for more.
ℹ️
Your specific situation — ovaries kept
Since your uterus was removed but your ovaries are still active, your hormones continue cycling naturally. You may not need HRT in the same way as someone whose ovaries have stopped working — but you may still benefit from targeted support for specific symptoms like vaginal dryness, low libido, or declining testosterone. This is worth discussing with a hormone-literate provider who understands your specific surgical history.
ℹ️
Your specific situation — natural menopause
Your ovaries wound down gradually rather than stopping suddenly, which means your body had more time to adapt than with surgical menopause. HRT for natural menopause is well-supported by evidence — particularly when started within 10 years of menopause or before age 60. The primary goals are long-term bone density, cardiovascular protection, brain health, and quality of life — not just symptom relief.
⚡
Surgical menopause is fundamentally different from natural menopause — your ovaries stopped producing estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone abruptly rather than gradually, typically within 24–48 hours of surgery rather than over several years.
💊
Bioidentical HRT — estrogen gel, testosterone gel, and micronized progesterone — replaces those hormones in forms your body recognizes. For surgical menopause specifically, major medical societies frame this less as an optional symptom treatment and more as replacing something your body lost suddenly and was not ready to lose.
🔬
This matters because the well-known 2002 WHI study that made many people fearful of HRT studied older women going through natural menopause — it did not specifically address younger women whose ovaries were surgically removed.
⚡
Testosterone is especially relevant after surgical menopause, since it is lost just as abruptly as estrogen and progesterone — but it is often left out of the conversation entirely. Low energy and reduced libido are common signs worth raising directly with your doctor. See the "Types" tab for more.

Setting the record straight on the 2002 WHI study
📰
What the study actually usedThe WHI used oral synthetic progestins and conjugated equine estrogen (from horse urine) — not bioidentical hormones. It studied women with an average age of 63, most of whom were more than 10 years past menopause. Its findings do not directly apply to bioidentical transdermal hormones started closer to menopause.
✅
Transdermal estrogen gel carries no increased clot riskMultiple large studies have found that transdermal estrogen — gel, patch, or spray — does not carry the blood clot risk associated with oral estrogen tablets. The route of delivery matters significantly.
✅
Bioidentical micronized progesterone has a better safety profileUnlike the synthetic progestins used in the WHI, bioidentical micronized progesterone does not appear to carry the same elevated breast cancer or cardiovascular risk. Several studies specifically show a more favorable profile.
What the positive evidence actually shows
✅
NAMS 2022: Benefits outweigh risks for most womenFor healthy women under 60 or within 10 years of menopause, the North American Menopause Society states that benefits of HRT outweigh risks for most women with bothersome symptoms.
✅
Significant bone density protectionHRT is one of the most effective interventions for preventing osteoporosis-related fractures. Estrogen directly supports bone remodeling — its decline is a primary driver of bone loss in menopause.
✅
Cardiovascular protection when started earlyEvidence supports a "timing hypothesis" — HRT started within 10 years of menopause or before age 60 may reduce cardiovascular risk rather than raise it. The risk profile changes when started later.
✅
Brain and cognitive protectionEstrogen plays a role in memory, cognitive function, and neuroprotection. Some research suggests starting HRT during the perimenopause window may support long-term brain health, including potential dementia risk reduction.
✅
Testosterone gel: real evidence for energy, libido, and moodBioidentical testosterone gel is the same approach used successfully by many women including those with surgical menopause. Guidelines from NICE and the British Menopause Society specifically support its use for low sexual desire. Many women also report meaningful improvements in energy, motivation, and cognitive clarity.
✅
Estrogen gel for symptom reliefBioidentical estrogen gel effectively reduces hot flashes, night sweats, sleep disruption, vaginal dryness, and mood instability. For many women these are not minor inconveniences — they are quality-of-life issues that affect work, relationships, and daily functioning.
✅
No arbitrary time limitNAMS and the British Menopause Society both state there is no evidence-based time limit on how long HRT can be used for women without contraindications. The old "5-year rule" was never grounded in evidence.
💡Estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone can all be delivered in gel form as bioidentical hormones — meaning some women manage their entire hormone protocol with gels alone, without patches or pills. Ask your provider what combination makes sense for you.

When levels drop, effects go beyond libido: persistent fatigue that sleep does not fix, mood changes, brain fog, and reduced muscle strength. Evidence supports its use in both natural and surgical menopause. Given transdermally as a gel or cream — some women use bioidentical estrogen and testosterone gels as part of the same daily protocol.

🔬
Estrogen and the brainEstrogen receptors are throughout the brain — including the limbic system, which processes emotion. Declining estrogen directly affects emotional regulation, memory, and stress response.
😴
Why sleep is disruptedProgesterone has a sedating effect. As it drops, so does sleep quality. Night sweats from vasomotor instability wake her from deep sleep — the restorative phase. This is cumulative.
⚡
Testosterone drops too — and it often goes unaddressedTestosterone starts declining in women as early as the mid-30s and continues through perimenopause. Low testosterone is behind much of the persistent fatigue, low libido, and motivation loss that estrogen and progesterone treatment alone does not fully resolve. It is frequently not tested or discussed — which is why many women still feel "off" even when other hormones are being treated.
🌡️
The cortisol connectionDeclining estrogen makes the HPA axis (stress response system) more reactive. The same stressor genuinely produces a stronger cortisol response than it would have before perimenopause.
💊
Why HRT matters — including testosteroneHRT is not about reversing aging. It is about replacing hormones that protect the brain, heart, bones, and quality of life. A complete picture includes all three hormones — not just estrogen and progesterone.
⚡
Why surgical menopause hits differentlyNatural menopause unfolds over 4-10 years. Surgical menopause happens in days. The brain and body do not have time to adapt gradually — they have to respond to a sudden, complete withdrawal of estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone simultaneously.
🧠
All three hormones drop — but the timeline is completely differentIn natural menopause, estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone all decline — but gradually over years, giving the body some time to adapt. In surgical menopause, all three drop within days. That abruptness is what makes the experience so much more intense, and why testosterone is often the missing piece even in natural menopause conversations.
💔
The grief piece is realResearch shows many women experience grief after hysterectomy that is not always acknowledged by medical teams or the people around them. This is not weakness — it is a legitimate psychological response to a significant physical and identity change.
💊
Why HRT matters more urgently hereFor surgical menopause before natural menopause age, major medical societies recommend HRT to protect long-term bone density and cardiovascular health — not just for symptom relief. The stakes are higher because the hormone loss was abrupt and complete.
🔬
A new, stable hormonal baselineUnlike perimenopause's fluctuations, post-menopause estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone have settled at consistently low levels. The wild swings are over — but the effects of low hormone levels persist.
🌡️
Hot flashes can continue for yearsFor some women, the brain's temperature-regulation center stays sensitized to low estrogen for a decade or more after the last period. That's normal — not a sign anything is wrong or that she is recovering slowly.
🦴
Bone and heart risk rise nowEstrogen's protective effect on bone density and cardiovascular health is gone at this stage. This is exactly why strength training and long-term health monitoring matter more here than in almost any other life stage.
💊
HRT remains an option, not a door that closedMany women benefit from starting or continuing bioidentical HRT well into post-menopause. It's worth an ongoing conversation with her doctor — not something that was only available during the transition itself.
